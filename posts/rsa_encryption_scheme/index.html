RSA encryption scheme

Posted on Jan 9, 2021

Algorithm

  1. Construction Phase:
    1. Select two random large primes $p$, and $q$.
    2. Calculate $N=pq$ and $\phi(N)=(p-1)(q-1)$.
    3. Pick an encryption key $e$ that is coprime with $\phi(N)$.
    4. Calculate the decryption key $d$ such that $$de\equiv 1\pmod{\phi(N)}.$$
  2. Operation Phase:
    1. Make the public key $(N, e)$ public, and keep $p$, $q$ and $d$ as secrets.
    2. Sender encrypts its message $M$ as $$E(M)=M^e\pmod{N}.$$
    3. Receiver decrypts it by calculating $$E(M)^d=M^{ed}\equiv M\pmod{N}.$$
